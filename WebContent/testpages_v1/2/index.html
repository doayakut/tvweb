Interesting facts about Transportation Vehicles

Vehicles of Transportation

Buses

A bus is a road vehicle designed to carry passengers.

Buses can have a capacity as high as 300 passengers.

Steam buses were used starting in in the 1830.

The first buses powered by internal combustion engines were used in 1895.

Bus is a clipped form of the Latin word Omnibus.

Trucks

A truck or lorry is a motor vehicle designed to transport cargo.

In the EU, vehicles with a mass of less than 3,500 kilograms are known as light commercial vehicles

In Australia and New Zealand, a pickup truck is sometimes referred to as ute

The cab is an enclosed space where the driver is seated.

A “sleeper” is a compartment attached to the cab where the driver can rest while not driving,

Trains

Earlier, almost all trains were powered by steam locomotives.

A train is a form of rail transport consisting of a series of vehicles propelled along a rail track to transport cargo or passengers

When referring to a train made up of a variety of vehicles, or of several sets/units, the term formation is used.

A bogie is a wheeled wagon or trolley.

Most bogies have two axles, as this is the simplest design.

Motorcycles

A motorcycle (also called a moto) is a two or three wheeled motor vehicle

There are around 200 million motorcycles in use worldwide

There are about 33 motorcycles per 1000 people

Most of the motorcycles, 58%, are in the developing countries of Asia

As of 2002, India, with an estimated 37 million motorcycles/mopeds, was home to the largest number of motorized two wheelers in the world.

Cars

The year of birth of the modern automobile is the year of 1866

The first working steam-powered car was designed by Ferdinand Verbiest around 1672.

It was a 65 cm-long scale-model toy for the Chinese Emperor, that was unable to carry a driver or a passenger

Nicolas-Joseph Cugnot is widely credited with building the first full-scale, self-propelled mechanical vehicle or automobile in about 1769.

He created a steam-powered tricycle.

Boats

A boat is a watercraft of any size designed to float and travel on water

In naval terms, a boat is a vessel small enough to be carried aboard on a ship.

Dugouts are the oldest boats archaeologists have found, dating back about eight thousand years

The oldest recovered boat in the world is the Pesse canoe

The sides of the boat is also called a gunnel

Bicycles

A bicycle a vehicle composed of two wheels, and propelled by pedals.

The first chain-driven model was developed around 1885.

Before 1868, and in some places thereafter, bikes were known primarily as velocipedes.

The first mechanically-propelled, two-wheeled vehicle may have been built by Kirkpatrick MacMillan, a Scottish blacksmith, in 1839.

He is associated with the first recorded instance of a cycling traffic offense when he knocked over a little girl in Glasgow and was fined five shillings.

Airplanes

An airplane is a powered fixed-wing aircraft that is propelled forward by thrust from a propeller.

Around 400 BC Archytas was reputed to have designed first self-propelled flying device

Sir George Cayley set forth the concept of the modern airplane in 1799

Sir Hiram Maxim built a craft that weighed 3.5 tons

The first jet airliner, the de Havilland Comet, was introduced in 1952.
