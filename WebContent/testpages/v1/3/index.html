We all have to drink water!

Brands of bottled water

Antipodes Water Company

Antipodes Water Company is a New Zealand water-bottling company.

It was established in 2003.

Antipodes water is sourced from an aquifer 327 meters below the ground in the Bay of Plenty.

In 2012, it won a silver in the carbonated bottled water category.

It is bottled at the source in Otakiri.

Aquafina

Aquafina waters are purified by going through a process called reverse osmosis.

Dasani is owned by PepsiCo.

It was first distributed in Wichita, Kansas in 1994.

The Aquafina brand includes flavored water; grape, strawberry kiwi, wild berry, raspberry, lemon, and peach mango.

As of 2009, Aquafina represented 13.4 % of domestic bottled water sales in the US.

Dasani

The first source of Dasani water was in Alberta.

Dasani is owned by The Coca-Cola Company.

The company was launched in 1999, after the success of Aquafina.

The Dasani brand includes flavored water; lemon, grape, raspberry, and strawberry.

Dasani was introduced to the Brazilian market in mid-2003.

Deer Park Spring Water

The original source of the water was a spring near Deer Park, Maryland.

It is a division of Nestle.

Deer Park Spring Water was established in 1873.

Deer Park's current slogan is "Born Better".

In 2006, the Aquapod was released under this brand.

Evian

Evian mineral waters comes from several sources near the south shore of Lake Geneva.

The pH of Evian waters is 7.2.

Evian is owned by a French multinational company.

Mineral Water company was founded in 1829.

The company entered into the U.S. market 1978.

Fiji

Fiji water comes from an artesian aquifer in the Yaqara Valley of Viti Levu.

The company is owned by Resnick family.

Fiji waters was started in 1996 by David Gilmour.

The pH of Fiji waters is 7.9.

In December 2010, Fiji Water's Fiji plant had 400 employees.

Mountain Valley Spring Water

Mountain Valley Spring Water is bottled in Hot Springs, Arkansas.

It has been bottled continuously since 1871.

Mountain Valley Spring Water is currently owned by Clear Mountain Spring Water Company of Little Rock, Arkansas.

In 2003, it received a Gold Medal at the Berkeley Springs International Water Tasting Awards for Non-Carbonated Bottled Water.

Following a heart attack in 1955, President Dwight Eisenhower drank the water on the advice of his physician

Nestle Pure Life

The first source of Nestle waters was Aberfoyle Springs.

Nestle Pure Life water is also sold in Canada.

The brand has been sold as Nestle Pure Life since 2002.

The pH of Nestle Pure Life waters is 7.5.

Nestle has 2 bottling facilities in Canada.
