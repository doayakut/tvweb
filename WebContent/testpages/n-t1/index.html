Wine Styles

Dessert wine

Dessert wines are sometimes called pudding wines.

Dessert wines are sweet wines typically served with dessert.

In the U.S., a dessert wine is defined as any wine over 14% alcohol by volume.

Makers of dessert wines want to produce a wine containing high levels of both sugar and alcohol.

Fruit wine

Fruit wines are fermented alcoholic beverages made from a variety of base ingredients.

Fruit wines have traditionally been popular with home winemakers.

Fruit wines are usually referred to by their main ingredient.

Fruit wine can be made from virtually any plant matter that can be fermented.

Orange wine

Orange wine can be one of three very different types of wine.

The term could refer to a beverage made by fermenting orange juice, rather than grape juice.

Typically orange wine production involves crushing the grapes.

Orange wines were common in Italy in the 1950s and 1960s.

Red wine

Red wine is a type of wine made from dark-coloured (black) grape varieties

The actual colour of the wine can range from intense violet through to brick red and brown.

The red colour comes from anthocyan pigments

Much of the red-wine production process involves extraction of colour and flavour
